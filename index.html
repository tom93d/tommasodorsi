Tommaso d'Orsi

Email: tommaso (dot) dorsi (at) unibocconi (dot) it

I recently finished my PhD at ETH Zurich in the Department of Computer Science, where I was fortunate to have David Steurer as my advisor.

In October 2023 I will start a post-doc at Bocconi hosted by Luca Trevisan.

During my PhD, I spent several months in the Graph Mining team, part of the Google AI Algorithms and Optimization team, Vincent Cohen-Addad was my host.

I am broadly interested in computer science, from both a theoretical and applied perspective. The bulk of my research lies somewhere in the intersection of algorithm design, learning theory, privacy and computational complexity. Most recently, I have been working on algorithmic aspects of differential privacy, fine-grain complexity, the statistical and computational aspects of random and semi-random models.
